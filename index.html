Welcome to ICCCA-2018

Conference Record # 41372

The University is established under Galgotias University Uttar Pradesh Act 14 of 2011. With a focus on multidisciplinary research, education and a learning model that emphasizes active learning, GU aspires to be globally known for innovation at the intersection of disciplines. GU’s bold vision builds upon over a decade of excellence of Galgotias Institutions in engineering and business education.

School of Computing Science and Engineering, Galgotias University is organizing, International Conference on Computing, Communication and Automation (ICCCA2018). The conference will take place from 5-6th May, 2018 at the university campus, located on the bank of Yamuna Expressway, Near Budhha International Circuit, Greater Noida, UP, India. ICCCA2018 is a non-profit conference with an aim to bring together the researchers, developers, students, academicians, and corporate practitioners to meet, exchange and derive the thoughts and future directions in Computing, Communication and Automation.

We have successfully organized the ICCCA-2015, ICCCA-2016 & ICCCA-2017. All registered and presented papers of ICCCA-2015 and ICCCA-2016 are available on IEEE Xplore digital library. ICCCA-2015 is also indexed in SCOPUS and GOOGLE SCHOLAR. Soon all registered and presented papers of ICCCA-2017 will be available on IEEE Xplore digital library.

The evolution of computing and Communications Technologies and their reliability in the different areas of the industry has changed completely the design of automated systems. Computing covers application of Computer Science to the mathematical modeling of complex systems in the field of Engineering and Sciences. Computing is interdisciplinary and application oriented, focusing on techniques & tools that enable computational simulations to be performed.

The vision today is to best utilize the media of communication. Communication processes are fundamental part of virtually every aspect of human computer interaction (HCI). Research on HCI or data communication takes you to the cutting-edge of research and theory in all areas within the field of communication like exploration of communication processes, antecedents & consequences of communication in a broad range of network communication.

According to the technological development and the new Computing and Communication technologies, and the race for competitiveness that drives the search for total quality and especially the reduction of costs , the structure of the computing and communication process has changed through the integration and automation of modern computer systems , who led the Application Programming Interface to be improvements of any kind whatsoever the number of features and especially the improvement of new industrial communication protocol such as Profibus, Profinet and Ethernet/IP. Automation includes theoretical and experimental research covering all facets of automatic control system, having a focal point analysis and design methods using tools of modeling, simulation and optimization. Specific areas of research include non linear, distributed, adaptive, stochastic and robust control. Application areas include automotive, process control, network control, multi agent, sensor network and control of computing system.

Taj Mahal
Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum located on the southern bank of the Yamuna River in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife of three, Mumtaz Mahal.

Red Fort
India Gate

The Red Fort was the residence of the Mughal emperor of India for nearly 200 years, until 1857. It is located in the centre of Delhi and houses a number of museums.

India Gate
India Gate

At the centre of New Delhi stands the 42 m high India Gate, an "Arc-de-Triomphe" like archway in the middle of a crossroad. Almost similar to its French counterpart, it commemorates the 70,000 Indian soldiers who lost their lives fighting for the British Army during the World War I. The memorial bears the names of more than 13,516 British and Indian soldiers killed in the Northwestern Frontier in the Afghan war of 1919.

Lotus Temple
Lotus Temple

East of Nehru place, this temple is built in the shape of a lotus flower and is the last of seven Major Bahai's temples built around the world. Completed in1986 it is set among the lush green landscaped gardens.

Akshardham
Akshardham

Swaminarayan Akshardham at New Delhi is a Mandir – an abode of God, a Hindu house of worship, and a spiritual and cultural campus dedicated to devotion, learning and harmony.

Read More
Qutab Minar
Qutab Minar

Qutab Minar is a soaring, 73 m-high tower of victory, built in 1193 by Qutab-ud-din Aibak immediately after the defeat of Delhi's last Hindu kingdom. The tower has five distinct storeys, each marked by a projecting balcony and tapers from a 15 m diameter at the base to just 2.5 m at the top. The first three storeys are made of red sandstone; the fourth and fifth storeys are of marble and sandstone. At the foot of the tower is the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque, the first mosque to be built in India.
